MediqWise Regulatory & Quality provides regulatory standards and guidance monitoring services for medical device, SaMD, and digital health organizations that need a practical way to stay informed of regulatory and standards changes.

The service is designed to help organizations monitor relevant standards, guidance documents, and regulatory updates across selected jurisdictions, identify meaningful changes, and translate those updates into clear monthly reporting and action-oriented summaries.

## What the service includes

### Regulatory standards and guidance monitoring

MediqWise monitors selected regulatory and standards sources for:

- Newly issued or revised guidance documents
- Newly recognized, published, revised, obsoleted, or withdrawn standards
- Standards under development or revision, where applicable
- Changes in regulatory expectations that may affect medical device or SaMD compliance activities

### Change identification and analysis

Identified updates are reviewed to determine whether they are likely to be relevant to the client’s product portfolio, regulatory markets, QMS, submissions, or post-market activities.

The analysis may include:

- Change description
- High-level relevance assessment
- Potential impact on regulatory submissions, technical documentation, QMS procedures, labelling, cybersecurity, usability, clinical activities, or post-market processes
- Suggested action items, where applicable

### Monthly regulatory monitoring report

The monthly report can be customized based on client needs and may include:

- Executive summary
- Regulatory signal assessment
- Standards status overview
- Newly published or newly recognized standards
- Foreseeable revisions, including standards under development
- Obsoleted or withdrawn standards
- Jurisdiction-based guidance updates
- Impact summary and recommendations
- Suggested action item tables
- Suggested applicability and compliance risk ratings

## Example monitoring scope

The monitoring scope can be tailored to the client’s markets, product types, and regulatory strategy. Common sources may include:

- United States FDA guidance documents and recognized consensus standards
- Health Canada medical device guidance and recognized standards
- European Union MDR / IVDR and MDCG guidance
- Official Journal of the European Union harmonised standards updates
- United Kingdom MHRA guidance and designated standards
- Australia TGA guidance and recognized standards
- New Zealand Medsafe updates
- IMDRF guidance documents
- New and revised ISO, IEC, AAMI, ANSI, and other relevant medical device standards

Jurisdictions and sources can be added or removed based on the client’s business needs.

## Example report outputs

A typical monthly report may identify whether there were:

- New or revised medical device standards
- Standards under development that may require future planning
- New or revised FDA, Health Canada, EU, MHRA, TGA, Medsafe, or IMDRF guidance
- Changes that may affect active or planned submissions
- Updates that may affect QMS procedures, labelling, usability engineering, cybersecurity, clinical evaluation, clinical investigation, vigilance, or post-market activities
- Updates that are administrative only and do not require formal action

Where appropriate, the report can include practical action tables with:

- Guidance or standard title
- Change description
- Suggested action items
- Suggested applicability
- Compliance risk rating

## Who this service is for

This service may be useful for organizations that:

- Need a structured regulatory intelligence process
- Sell or plan to sell medical devices, SaMD, or digital health products in multiple markets
- Need periodic standards and guidance monitoring for QMS or management review purposes
- Want support identifying which updates are meaningful and which are informational only
- Need practical summaries rather than raw regulatory update lists
- Have limited internal capacity for ongoing monitoring

## Customization options

The service can be customized based on:

- Product type, including SaMD, SiMD, AI-enabled medical devices, imaging software, connected devices, or traditional medical devices
- Jurisdictions of interest
- Applicable standards and guidance lists
- Reporting frequency
- Level of detail required
- Internal review format
- Whether action item tables, risk ratings, or impact summaries are needed

## Initial setup

During the initial setup phase, MediqWise works with the client to confirm:

- Applicable jurisdictions and sources
- Product and technology scope
- Reference standards and guidance lists
- Reporting structure
- Desired level of detail
- Internal terminology and preferred report format

Once the monitoring approach and reporting format are stabilized, the service may be provided as an ongoing monthly monitoring activity.
